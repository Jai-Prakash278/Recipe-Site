French Break Pizza

French Bread Pizza is a quick and delicious way to enjoy homemade pizza with a crispy crust and gooey toppings. Using a loaf of French bread as the base, this recipe is perfect for a fast weeknight meal or a fun snack. Simply top with your favorite sauce, cheese, and toppings, then bake until golden and bubbly!

Ingredients:

  • 1 loaf of French bread (sliced in half lengthwise)
  • 1 cup pizza sauce
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup pepperoni slices (or preferred toppings)
  • 1/2 teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Optional: bell peppers, mushrooms, olives, onions, or fresh basil

The highlight of a French bread pizza is its thick, crunchy crust with a light crumb. To achieve this texture, you must ensure the pizza is baked at the right temperature.

Brick Oven Pizza

Brick Oven Pizza is known for its crispy, charred crust and rich, smoky flavor, thanks to the high heat of a traditional brick oven. This pizza cooks quickly, resulting in a perfectly crispy yet chewy texture with bubbly cheese and bold toppings. It's a classic choice for an authentic pizzeria experience right at home!

Ingredients:

  • 1 ball of pizza dough (homemade or store-bought)
  • 1/2 cup pizza sauce
  • 1 and 1/2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 cup sliced pepperoni or other toppings of choice
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Optional: fresh basil, cherry tomatoes, mushrooms, or red pepper flakes

A Brick oven pizza gets cooked by the high temperature achieved by employing burning wood, so make sure the woods you‚Äôre using are properly lit and of good quality for maximum flavors.

Neapolitan Pizza

Neapolitan Pizza is a classic Italian pizza known for its thin, soft, and slightly charred crust, cooked at high temperatures in a wood-fired oven. With simple, fresh ingredients, this pizza delivers an authentic taste of Naples, featuring a light and airy dough topped with rich tomato sauce, fresh mozzarella, and fragrant basil.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ball of Neapolitan pizza dough (00 flour, water, yeast, salt)
  • 1/2 cup San Marzano tomato sauce
  • 1 and 1/2 cups fresh mozzarella (Fior di Latte or Buffalo)
  • 4-5 fresh basil leaves
  • 1/2 teaspoon sea salt
  • 1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil
  • Optional: grated Parmesan, cherry tomatoes, or a drizzle of balsamic reduction

If you want to enjoy an authentic Neapolitan pizza, make sure you make the base using refined wheat flour and bake it for 60 to 90 seconds at 700 ¬∞ to 1000 ¬∞ F.

Italian Pizza

Italian Pizza is a delicious and authentic dish that showcases the simplicity and richness of traditional Italian ingredients. With a thin, flavorful crust, fresh tomato sauce, creamy mozzarella, and aromatic herbs, this pizza is a staple of Italian cuisine. Cooked to perfection, it offers a balance of crispiness and chewiness in every bite.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ball of pizza dough (00 flour, water, yeast, salt)
  • 1/2 cup homemade tomato sauce (San Marzano tomatoes, garlic, olive oil)
  • 1 and 1/2 cups fresh mozzarella cheese
  • 1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 4-5 fresh basil leaves
  • Optional: prosciutto, mushrooms, olives, or Parmesan cheese

To ensure you get the perfect Italian pizza, it is essential that the oven is adequately hot and has hit the right temperature. Else the pizza may taste undercooked.

Chicago Style Pizza

Chicago-Style Pizza, also known as deep-dish pizza, is a hearty and indulgent dish with a thick, buttery crust that holds layers of cheese, rich tomato sauce, and delicious toppings. Baked in a deep pan, this pizza is known for its crispy edges, gooey center, and signature upside-down layering of ingredients.

Ingredients:

  • ball of deep-dish pizza dough (flour, cornmeal, butter, yeast, salt)
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 and 1/2 cups crushed San Marzano tomatoes
  • 1/2 pound Italian sausage (or other preferred toppings)
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Optional: bell peppers, onions, mushrooms, or black olives

The best way to prepare a Chicago Style Pizza is to layer the ingredients in the correct order properly. Adequate oil should also be placed in the pan, so the thick pizza sides get properly baked.

California Pizza

California Pizza is a fresh and creative take on traditional pizza, known for its unique gourmet toppings and lighter crust. Combining the best of Italian tradition with Californian innovation, this pizza often features fresh, locally sourced ingredients like avocado, arugula, and goat cheese for a vibrant and flavorful experience.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ball of pizza dough (thin or hand-tossed)
  • 1/2 cup tomato sauce or pesto
  • 1 and 1/2 cups shredded mozzarella or goat cheese
  • 1/2 cup grilled chicken or smoked salmon (optional)
  • 1/2 sliced avocado (for topping after baking)
  • 1/4 cup sun-dried tomatoes or cherry tomatoes
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Optional: arugula, caramelized onions, roasted garlic, or balsamic glaze

If you want to get maximum flavors from your California pizza, try baking it in a wood-fired oven instead of the conventional oven. You will feel the difference in flavors yourself.

Sicilian Pizza

Sicilian Pizza is a thick-crust, square-cut pizza known for its light and airy texture with a crispy bottom. Originating from Sicily, this pizza features a rich tomato sauce, generous cheese, and often anchovies or other bold toppings. Its focaccia-like crust makes it a hearty and satisfying choice.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ball of Sicilian pizza dough (high-hydration dough with olive oil)
  • 1 cup tomato sauce (San Marzano tomatoes, garlic, olive oil)
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (for greasing the pan)
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Optional: anchovies, black olives, onions, or pepperoni

To bake a perfectly authentic Sicilian pizza, make sure the pizza sauce used is without any meat, and it is baked at the perfect temperature. You can bake it in any conventional oven if a brick wood oven isn‚Äôt available.

Greek Pizza

Greek Pizza is a flavorful and Mediterranean-inspired dish featuring a thick, chewy crust baked in an oiled pan for a crispy edge. Topped with tangy tomato sauce, feta cheese, and a variety of fresh ingredients like olives and oregano, this pizza offers a deliciously unique twist on traditional styles.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ball of Greek pizza dough (all-purpose flour, olive oil, yeast, salt)
  • 1/2 cup tomato sauce (or a garlic-olive oil base)
  • 1 and 1/2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup sliced Kalamata olives
  • 1/4 cup diced red onions
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Optional: sliced tomatoes, spinach, bell peppers, or banana peppers

To bake a Greek pizza to perfection, make sure to use a deep pan for baking the pizza. You can also use a generous amount of olive oil to give it an authentic touch.

New York Style Pizza

New York-Style Pizza is famous for its large, thin, and foldable slices with a crispy yet chewy crust. Baked at high temperatures, this pizza features a balanced layer of tangy tomato sauce, gooey mozzarella cheese, and classic toppings, making it a go-to street food in the Big Apple.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ball of New York-style pizza dough (high-gluten flour, water, yeast, salt)
  • 1/2 cup tomato sauce (San Marzano tomatoes, garlic, olive oil)
  • 2 cups shredded low-moisture mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (for brushing the crust)
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Optional: pepperoni, mushrooms, sausage, or red pepper flakes

If you want to enjoy a New York style pizza loaded with toppings, bake it in the 500-600 degrees range. This temperature is required to ensure all the toppings, sauces, and cheese are properly baked.

Detroit Pizza

Detroit Pizza is a thick, rectangular pizza known for its crispy, caramelized cheese edges and airy, pillowy crust. Baked in a deep, well-oiled pan, this pizza features a unique layering of cheese first, followed by toppings, and finished with tomato sauce on top for a signature look and taste.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ball of Detroit-style pizza dough (high-hydration dough with olive oil)
  • 2 cups shredded Wisconsin brick cheese (or mozzarella and cheddar blend)
  • 1 cup tomato sauce (added after baking)
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (for greasing the pan)
  • 1/2 cup sliced pepperoni (or other toppings)
  • Optional: caramelized onions, mushrooms, banana peppers, or sausage

To bake the perfect Detroit pizza, set the oven temperature to 500 ¬∞F to 550 ¬∞F. Make sure the oven you‚Äôre using is as hot as it can get, so the pizza crust gets baked properly. The browning and crisping of the crust take place at high temperatures, so make sure to not avoid it.

Bagel Pizza

Bagel Pizza is a fun and easy twist on traditional pizza, using bagels as the crust. Perfect for a quick snack or meal, the bagels are topped with marinara sauce, cheese, and your favorite toppings, then baked until the cheese is melted and bubbly. It‚Äôs a delicious, bite-sized pizza that's great for kids and adults alike.

Ingredients:

  • 2 plain bagels (sliced in half)
  • 1/2 cup pizza sauce or marinara sauce
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/4 cup sliced pepperoni or other toppings
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (for brushing the bagels)
  • Optional: bell peppers, onions, mushrooms, or fresh basil

To ensure the bagel pizza is perfectly baked, carefully place all the toppings and let it bake in the oven till the cheese completely melts. You can bake a bagel pizza in a conventional oven, brick-oven pizza, or even microwave it.
